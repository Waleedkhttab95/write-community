# Project Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
A broad Arabic-speaking community centered on the written word: aspiring writers, established
writers, readers, and partners/sponsors. The site (مجتمع الكتابة — "Writing Community") is the
public face of a writing-skills organization. Visitors come to explore programs, workshops,
tracks, and meetups; read published articles; meet partners; and get in touch or subscribe.
The interface is **RTL Arabic-first** (`lang="ar" dir="rtl"`).

### Brand Personality
Refined & literary. Voice: elegant, editorial, calm — evoking craft, heritage, and the
prestige of the written word. Three words: **refined, literary, warm**. The interface should
make a visitor feel they are entering a place that respects language and craftsmanship —
confident but never loud.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Palette**: warm earth tones already defined as brand tokens — gold `#AA8A52`
  (light `#C6B088`, dark `#8E7038`), red `#871F22`, orange `#DD7619`, brown `#77523F`.
  Dominant warm neutrals with gold as the signature accent; red/orange used sparingly.
- **Typography**: Brando Arabic as the primary display/body face, with Tajawal as an Arabic
  fallback. Avoid the decorative Google faces (Monoton, Tilt Neon) for anything substantive.
- **Theme**: both light and dark must be supported. Light is the primary experience (warm
  off-white backgrounds, not pure white); dark mode must be a real, audited mode.
- **Anti-references**: no AI-slop tells — no cyan/purple gradients, no glassmorphism, no
  gradient text on headings/metrics, no endless identical card grids, no generic drop-shadow
  rounded rectangles.

### Design Principles
1. **Editorial restraint** — generous whitespace, clear type hierarchy, asymmetry over
   centered-everything; let the writing breathe.
2. **Warm, tinted neutrals** — never pure black/white; tint neutrals toward gold/brown for
   subconscious cohesion.
3. **Gold as signature, used sparingly** — accents earn attention; avoid flooding the page
   with gradients or color.
4. **RTL-correct and accessible** — every layout, icon direction, and focus order must work
   in Arabic RTL and meet WCAG 2.1 AA (contrast ≥ 4.5:1, keyboard nav, semantic landmarks).
5. **Intentional motion** — purposeful entrances and feedback with smooth (non-bounce)
   easing; respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.

### Accessibility Target
WCAG 2.1 AA.
